Fellow @ SHAD

I had the incredible opportunity to attend the 2024 Shad Canada program at Ontario Tech University. Over the course of a month, I collaborated with bright, driven students from across the country on a real-world challenge—and our team ended up winning first place in the Design and Entrepreneurial Project. Our startup, ChillCharge, focused on reducing fossil fuel consumption in northern regions of Canada, specifically Nunavut. We designed a waste heat capturing system that transports residual heat from power plants into a greenhouse containing nearly 6,000 thermoelectric generators. By leveraging the extreme temperature difference—waste heat inside and freezing temperatures outside—we were able to generate enough electricity to power 1,000 homes. It was an ambitious, bold idea that pushed the boundaries of sustainable energy
